## Project Overview

Lux HR is a consulting firm that helps small to mid-sized businesses improve their HR operations, team development, and internal systems.

The goal of this project was to design a professional, modern website that communicates complex HR services in a way that feels clear, trustworthy, and human — while also increasing client inquiries.

## The Problem

Most HR consulting websites fall into one of two extremes: overly corporate and cold (difficult to connect with emotionally), or too vague and generic (unclear services and value).

Lux HR's initial challenges included:

- No strong digital presence
- Services not clearly structured
- No clear user journey or conversion path
- Lack of visual identity consistency

This created friction for potential clients trying to understand what Lux HR offers, decide if the company is trustworthy, and take action.

## The Goal

Design a website that:

- Communicates HR services clearly and simply
- Establishes trust within seconds
- Feels professional but approachable
- Guides users toward a clear action — "Work With Us"

## My Role

**UI/UX Designer + Visual Designer**

Responsibilities included UX structure and user flow, wireframing and layout design, visual identity direction, and responsive UI design.

## Design Approach

### 1. Understanding the User

The primary users are small to mid-sized business owners, HR managers needing support, and founders looking for scalable systems.

Key user questions:
- "Can I trust this company?"
- "Do they understand my business?"
- "What exactly do they offer?"
- "How do I get started?"

### 2. Competitive Analysis

I reviewed multiple HR consulting websites and identified common issues: dense paragraphs of jargon-heavy text, poor visual hierarchy, lack of emotional connection, and weak or hidden calls-to-action.

**Opportunity:** Create a clean, structured, human-first experience that simplifies HR services.

### 3. Information Architecture

I simplified the entire site structure into four core sections: Home, Services, About, and Contact.

Instead of overwhelming users, the focus was on clear sections, predictable navigation, and logical content flow.

### 4. User Flow Design

The core journey follows: **Landing → Understanding → Trust → Action**

- **Hero Section** — Clear message about what Lux HR does with an immediate CTA
- **Services Section** — Offerings broken into digestible categories with simplified language
- **About Section** — Building credibility and human connection
- **Final CTA** — Reinforcing action with clear next steps

### 5. Wireframing

Structured layouts focused on clear hierarchy, readable spacing, and section-based storytelling.

Key decisions:
- Split hero layout (text + image) for balance
- Card-based services for easy scanning
- Repeated CTA placement throughout

### 6. Visual Design System

**Color Palette:** Warm neutrals (browns, creams) chosen for trust, stability, and approachability.

**Typography:** Strong heading hierarchy with clean, readable body text focused on clarity over decoration.

**UI Components:** Rounded cards for an approachable feel, soft shadows for depth without heaviness, and consistent button styling.

### 7. Designing for Trust

Trust is everything in HR. Trust was reinforced through a clean uncluttered layout, consistent branding, professional imagery, and clear confident language.

### 8. Conversion Strategy

Instead of a single CTA, strategic repetition was used:
- Hero → "Work With Us"
- Services → "Work With Us"
- Footer → "Let's Talk"

This ensures users can act at any point in their journey.

## Final Solution

The final website is clean and structured, easy to navigate, visually warm but professional, and focused on guiding users toward action. It balances business credibility, human connection, and clear usability.

## Impact & Results

While early-stage, the design achieved:

- Stronger brand identity
- Improved readability and content clarity
- More intuitive navigation
- Higher perceived trust and professionalism

## Key Takeaways

- Simplicity is powerful when dealing with complex services
- Trust is built through both design and structure
- Repetition of CTAs increases conversion opportunities
- Human-centered design makes professional services more accessible
