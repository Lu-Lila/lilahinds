## The Challenge

HR consulting websites tend to fall into two traps: either they're so corporate they feel cold and impersonal, or they're so vague that visitors can't tell what the company actually does. Lux HR — a consultancy helping small and mid-sized businesses with HR operations, team development, and internal systems — had no strong digital presence and needed a site that could bridge this gap.

The core problems were straightforward but interconnected. Services weren't clearly structured. There was no defined user journey. The visual identity was inconsistent. Potential clients couldn't easily understand what Lux HR offered, whether to trust them, or how to take the next step.

## Approach

The design started with the audience: business owners, HR managers, and founders who are busy, skeptical, and looking for quick answers to three questions — *What do you do? Can I trust you? How do I start?*

A competitive analysis of HR consulting sites revealed a pattern of dense jargon, poor visual hierarchy, and buried calls-to-action. The opportunity was clear: simplify everything.

**Information architecture** was reduced to four core sections — Home, Services, About, and Contact — with a user flow designed around a single narrative arc: **Land → Understand → Trust → Act.**

The hero section states what Lux HR does and provides an immediate CTA. The services section breaks offerings into scannable cards with simplified language. The about section builds human connection and credibility. And the closing CTA reinforces the next step.

## Visual Design

The visual system was built around trust and approachability. Warm neutrals — browns and creams — establish stability without feeling sterile. Typography uses a strong heading hierarchy with clean body text, prioritizing clarity over decoration. Rounded cards and soft shadows keep the interface approachable without sacrificing professionalism.

The services section showcases the card-based layout with clear categorization — Small to Mid Sized Business Support, Team Development, and Special Projects — each with a direct "Work With Us" CTA:

![Lux HR — Services Section](/images/lux-hr/services.png)

## Conversion Strategy

Rather than relying on a single call-to-action, the design uses strategic repetition: "Work With Us" appears in the hero and each service card, while "Let's Talk" anchors the footer. This ensures visitors can act at any point in their journey without scrolling back.

The dedicated services page extends this with tiered consulting packages — Entry Level, Mid Level, and Premium Plan — giving users clear options based on their needs:

![Lux HR — Services Page](/images/lux-hr/info-page.png)

## Outcome

The redesign delivered a stronger brand identity, improved content clarity and readability, more intuitive navigation, and a measurably more professional, trustworthy presence. The key insight: when services are complex, design simplicity isn't a shortcut — it's the strategy.
