## The Challenge

Salt Tampa Outreach serves the unhoused community in the Tampa Bay area through mobile outreach — providing showers, meals, clothing, barber services, and support resources. Their mission is deeply human, but their website wasn't. It was text-heavy, visually inconsistent, and buried the very actions (donate, volunteer) that keep the organization running.

Five problems needed solving: **information overload** that made it hard to grasp what SALT does; **no emotional hook** — the site read like a brochure, not a call to action; **buried CTAs** with no clear path from landing to engagement; **inconsistent design** that eroded credibility; and **missing identity** — the people behind the work were invisible.

## The Narrative Shift

The most important design decision was reframing the site from informational to story-driven. Instead of explaining what SALT does through long paragraphs, the redesign leads with visuals and emotion, then layers in detail progressively. The guiding principle: **show impact first, explain later.**

The site structure follows a deliberate narrative arc:

**Hero → Services → Mission → Team → Partners → Testimonials → Donate**

Each section builds on the previous one, mirroring the visitor's psychological journey: *feel something → understand the work → trust the people → take action.*

## Walking Through the Design

**Emotion** — The hero opens with a full-width aerial photograph of Tampa and a bold headline that grounds visitors in place and purpose. A live countdown to the next outreach event creates urgency, and the primary CTA — "Volunteer Today" — is immediately visible.

![Hero section with full-width Tampa aerial, countdown timer, and Volunteer Today CTA](/salt-hero.png)

**Understanding** — The services section replaces dense text with visual cards featuring real photography from outreach events. Visitors can quickly scan what SALT offers — clothing distribution, barber services, refreshments — without reading paragraphs. Dual CTAs for volunteering and contacting maintain momentum.

![Services section with photo cards for Clothes, Barber, and refreshment services](/salt-services.png)

**Trust** — The team section puts real faces to the organization. Featuring team members with their roles creates personal connection and accountability. The partners section beneath reinforces credibility through organizational affiliations.

![Team members section with photos and roles, followed by partner logos](/salt-team.png)

**Action** — The donation section is designed as a full-width emotional moment. A group photo of the SALT team at work sits behind bold text: *"We can continue to serve the Tampa Bay community with your help."* The donate CTA is unmissable.

![Full-width donation CTA with team photo and bold messaging](/salt-donate.png)

## Visual Language

The design language was built around warmth, trust, and urgency. Warm orange tones convey compassion and energy, used strategically for CTAs and accents. Neutral backgrounds maintain readability. Dark sections create visual weight for key moments like testimonials and the donation area.

Every photograph is real, taken from actual outreach events. This authenticity is the design's most powerful asset — it grounds the entire experience in the reality of the work being done.

The testimonials section anchors the page with SALT's mission statement set against outreach photography, serving as both social proof and a final emotional touchpoint.

![Testimonials section with mission statement over outreach photography](/salt-testimonials.png)

## Full Page Flow

The complete homepage reads as one continuous narrative — from emotional hook through understanding, trust-building, and finally the call to act. Every section earns the next scroll.

![Full page view of the redesigned Salt Tampa Outreach website](/salt-full-page.png)

## Accessibility

The redesign maintains high contrast ratios for readability, clear persistent navigation, mobile-responsive layouts that preserve hierarchy, and reduced cognitive load through visual chunking and whitespace.

## Outcome

The redesign delivered stronger emotional engagement through real photography and story-driven layout, clearer service communication via scannable cards, improved trust signals with visible team members and partners, and more prominent conversion paths at every stage of the journey. The central takeaway: nonprofit design is about emotion first, information second — visitors need to feel the mission before they read about it.
