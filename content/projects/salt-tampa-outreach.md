## Project Overview

Salt Tampa Outreach is a nonprofit organization dedicated to serving the unhoused community in the Tampa Bay area. Through mobile outreach programs, they provide essentials like showers, meals, clothing, barber services, and support resources — all driven by a mission to share compassion and catalyze permanent housing solutions.

The existing website was outdated and failed to convey the urgency and warmth of SALT's on-the-ground work. This project focused on a complete redesign — transforming a text-heavy, low-conversion site into a story-driven digital experience that builds emotional connection and moves visitors toward action.

## The Problem

The original website suffered from five core issues that undermined the organization's mission online:

**Information overload.** Dense paragraphs of text with no visual hierarchy made it difficult for visitors to quickly grasp what SALT does or how to get involved.

**No emotional hook.** The content read like an informational brochure rather than a call to action. There was no visual storytelling to convey the real human impact of SALT's outreach work.

**Buried calls-to-action.** Donation and volunteer opportunities were tucked away with no prominence. The user journey from landing to action was unclear and full of friction.

**Inconsistent design.** The layout lacked structure — inconsistent spacing, no focal points, and minimal use of modern UI patterns eroded trust and credibility.

**Missed identity.** SALT's team members, partners, and community presence were not showcased, leaving the site feeling impersonal and disconnected from the people behind the work.

## The Goal

Redesign the website to:

- Lead with emotion and impact before explanation
- Clearly communicate services in a scannable format
- Build trust through real photography and team visibility
- Create a direct path from engagement to action (donate, volunteer)
- Establish a modern, credible digital presence worthy of the mission

## My Role

**UI/UX Designer** — responsible for UX redesign strategy, information architecture, wireframing, visual UI design, and content structuring.

## Design Approach

### Reframing the Narrative

The most important shift was moving away from an informational layout toward a **story-driven experience**. Instead of explaining what SALT does in long paragraphs, the redesign leads with visuals and emotion, then layers in detail progressively.

The guiding principle: **"Show impact first, explain later."**

### Information Architecture

I restructured the entire site into a clear, intentional flow:

**Hero → Services → Mission → Team → Partners → Testimonials → Donate**

Each section has a single purpose and builds on the one before it, creating a narrative arc that mirrors the user journey: *feel something → understand the work → trust the people → take action.*

### User Flow: Emotion → Understanding → Trust → Action

The redesigned homepage walks visitors through four psychological stages:

**Emotion** — The hero section opens with a full-width aerial photograph of Tampa and a bold headline, immediately grounding the visitor in place and purpose. A live countdown to the next outreach event creates urgency, and the primary CTA — "Volunteer Today" — is front and center.

![Hero section with full-width Tampa aerial, countdown timer, and Volunteer Today CTA](/salt-hero.png)

**Understanding** — The services section replaces dense text with visual cards, each featuring real photography from outreach events. Visitors can quickly scan what SALT offers — clothing distribution, barber services, and refreshments — without wading through paragraphs. Dual CTAs for volunteering and contacting keep the momentum going.

![Services section with photo cards for Clothes, Barber, and refreshment services](/salt-services.png)

**Trust** — The team section puts real faces to the organization. Featuring Nahiel, Andrew, Oscar, Holly, and other team members with their roles creates personal connection and accountability. The partners section beneath reinforces credibility through organizational affiliations.

![Team members section with photos and roles, followed by partner logos](/salt-team.png)

**Action** — The donation section is designed as a full-width emotional moment. A group photo of the SALT team at work sits behind bold text: *"We can continue to serve the Tampa Bay community with your help."* The donate CTA is unmissable.

![Full-width donation CTA with team photo and bold messaging](/salt-donate.png)

### Visual Design System

The visual language was built to feel warm, trustworthy, and action-oriented:

**Color palette** — Warm orange tones convey compassion and energy, used strategically for CTAs and accent elements. Neutral backgrounds maintain readability, while dark sections create visual contrast and weight for key moments like the testimonials and donation areas.

**Typography** — Bold, clear headings establish hierarchy at a glance. Body text remains clean and readable, with generous spacing to reduce cognitive load.

**Photography** — Every image is real, taken from actual SALT outreach events. This authenticity is the design's most powerful asset — it shows rather than tells, and it grounds the entire experience in the reality of the work.

### Testimonials & Mission Reinforcement

The testimonials section anchors the page with SALT's full mission statement set against a compelling photograph of outreach in action. This section serves as both social proof and a final emotional touchpoint before the footer, reinforcing why the work matters.

![Testimonials section with mission statement over outreach photography](/salt-testimonials.png)

### Full Page Overview

The complete redesigned homepage flows as one continuous narrative — from the emotional hero through services, mission, team, partners, and finally the donation call-to-action. Every section earns the next scroll.

![Full page view of the redesigned Salt Tampa Outreach website](/salt-full-page.png)

## Accessibility & Usability

- High contrast ratios between text and backgrounds for readability
- Clear, persistent navigation with logical section labels
- Mobile-responsive layout that maintains hierarchy on smaller screens
- Reduced cognitive load through visual chunking and whitespace

## Impact

The redesign achieved:

- **Stronger emotional engagement** through real photography and story-driven layout
- **Clearer service communication** via scannable visual cards instead of text walls
- **Improved trust signals** with visible team members and partner affiliations
- **More prominent conversion paths** — donate and volunteer CTAs appear at every stage of the journey
- **Elevated brand perception** that matches the quality and heart of SALT's on-the-ground work

## Key Takeaways

- **Nonprofit design is about emotion first, information second.** Visitors need to feel the mission before they read about it.
- **Visual storytelling outperforms text.** Real photography from the field builds more trust than any copy could.
- **Repeated CTAs increase conversion.** Strategic placement at every scroll depth ensures visitors can act whenever they're ready.
- **Simplifying content doesn't mean removing it.** It means restructuring it so the right information appears at the right moment.

## What I'd Improve Next

- Optimize the donation flow with a multi-step UX to reduce drop-off
- Implement analytics tracking to measure engagement and conversion
- Add individual volunteer and donor testimonials for deeper social proof
- Build interactive impact metrics showing meals served, showers provided, and lives touched
